What is Farmyard Manure?

A decomposed mixture of dung, urine, bedding, and leftover fodder. Properly prepared FYM improves soil structure, nutrient availability and crop health while reducing the need for synthetic fertilisers.Manure is primarily derived from animal feces, used as agricultural fertilizer. It enriches soil with nutrients, improves soil structure, and promotes beneficial microbial activity, leading to healthier plant growth and increased crop yields.

Preparation

Collect urine-soaked litter on shed floors overnight. Combine with dung in trenches and plaster with slurry for controlled decomposition.

Application Timing

Partly rotted: 3–4 weeks before sowing. Well-rotted: immediately after sowing. Vegetables: at least 2 weeks before planting.

Loss Prevention

Incorporate manure quickly after spreading and avoid long field heaps to minimise volatilization and leaching losses.

Trench-based Composting

Standard trench dimensions and stepwise filling ensure uniform decomposition and controlled moisture. If urine collection is difficult, collect shed washings in a pit and add to the main batch.

Fertlizers that use crops

Crops such as cereals (rice, wheat, maize) need fertilizers to boost growth and grain production since they demand high nitrogen. Root and tuber crops (potato, carrot, beetroot) require balanced NPK to develop strong roots. Oilseeds (sunflower, mustard, groundnut) depend on phosphorus and potassium for better oil content and flowering. Vegetables (tomato, onion, cabbage) need continuous nutrient supply to support fast, heavy growth. Fruit crops (banana, mango, citrus) need regular fertilization to maintain soil fertility and produce high-quality fruits. Cash crops like sugarcane and cotton require steady nitrogen and potassium due to long growth cycles and high nutrient removal.

Trench dimensions

Depth: 1 metre
Width: 2 metres
Length: 7.5 metres
Time to maturity: 4–5 months

Best practices

  • Spread refuse to absorb urine overnight.
  • Plaster trenches with cow-dung slurry to retain moisture.
  • Incorporate manure into soil and plough immediately to reduce N loss.

Ideal Crops

Vegetables (carrot, tomato, potato, radish, onion), rice, sugarcane, napier grass, coconut, mango, banana, orange.

Quick facts

Nitrogen in urine is urea and vulnerable to volatilization — manage application timing accordingly.
